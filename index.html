What the Sprint is

Six things that keep you building.

The Sprint isn't a course you watch. It's a practice you keep, with people who keep it alongside you.

  • 01

    A daily practice that finally sticks

    Three one-month writing challenges: fifteen minutes a day of undistracted writing, with a group that's counting on you to show up. It's optional, but it's the simplest way to turn good intentions into a habit you keep.

  • 02

    Learn from people who've done it

    Eight live workshops across the Sprint with speakers from the 2026 summit. Every session is recorded, so a missed evening is never a missed lesson.

  • 03

    A room that stays warm

    A two-hour community call each month, where you bring the grievances, goals, and small wins that creators usually carry alone.

  • 04

    Support for the lonely parts

    A growing pool of mental well-being resources for the isolation of digital entrepreneurship, because you shouldn't have to face the hard days by yourself.

  • 05

    A small group that notices you

    Your accountability pod: four to six creators who meet weekly and hold each other to their goals. You'll be placed alongside people from the 100-person 2026 summit and other new registrants, grouped by time zone, creative focus, and who you ask to be with.

  • 06

    A home base that never closes

    An online community with spaces to chat, share work in progress, and trade feedback between calls. It's also where the full recordings of the 2026 Future-Proof Creator Summit live, yours to watch from the very start. The free spaces, like the maker space chat, stay open to you even after your three months of Premium membership lapse.

An honest fit

Who this is for.

The Sprint asks something of you. It's worth being honest about whether that's what you're looking for right now.

This is for you if

  • You make things, and you want a daily practice that outlasts your motivation.
  • You're tired of building alone and want a small group that notices when you go quiet.
  • You'd rather do the work with people than watch another course by yourself.
  • You felt something at the summit, or you wish you'd been there, and you want to keep that momentum.

This isn't for you if

  • You want a course to binge alone and never speak to anyone.
  • You're after overnight growth hacks or a get-rich-quick promise.
  • You have no capacity to show up for a group over the next ninety days.

Questions

Good to know.

Do I need to have attended the 2026 summit?
No. The Sprint is open to any creator, whether or not you were in the room in 2026.
What if I can't make a workshop live?
Everything is recorded. All eight workshops and the monthly calls are available to watch later, on your own time.
When do the live sessions happen?
Workshops are usually Thursday evenings and Saturday mornings, Pacific time. The community-wide networking calls, open to every Premium member, happen once a month and run two hours or more. All times are PDT.
Is the writing challenge mandatory?
No, it's optional. It's fifteen minutes of undistracted writing a day, done at whatever time suits you, with a shared checklist so everyone can see each other's streaks and keep each other going.
What happens after the 90 days?
At the end of September your Premium membership lapses. You can renew at new pricing for a lighter, stay-connected set of offerings. The free community spaces, like the maker space chat, stay open to you either way.
What's your refund policy?
If you're unhappy in the first month, just let us know and we'll refund you in full.
How are the accountability pods grouped?
By time zone, creative focus area, and any requests to be grouped with specific people. You'll fill out a short intake survey after you register, which arrives by email.
